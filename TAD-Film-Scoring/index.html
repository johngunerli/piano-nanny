Animation Scoring

What We Do That Makes a Difference
All of the visual movements in a scene (walking, hand gestures, head turns, transitions, etc) contribute to a rhythmical pattern. We tap into this pattern and translate it into a tempo map so that the music fits the scene like a glove. We do the same for audiobooks, using the narration performance.

This is the first art of film scoring and transforms, what appears to be a set of songs, into a synchronized score. Approaching the music in this way allows the film director all the freedom they need to edit in a way that best tells the story; then the music is rhythmically and texturally designed to fit that story.
